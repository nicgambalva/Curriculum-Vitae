# Nicolas Gamboa's Cover Letter & CV Preferences

## Cover Letter Style Guidelines

### Formatting & Structure
- **No dashes or numbered lists** - avoid bullet points like (1), (2), (3) or using "-" for listing items
- **Simplified company names** - use "UBS" not "UBS Europe SE — Advisory & Sales"
- **Simplified legal names** - use the general company name without legal entities (e.g., "TotalEnergies Trading" not "TotalEnergies Trading Asia Pte Ltd")
- **Simplified role titles** - use the core role name without site qualifiers (e.g., "Trading Graduate Program 2026" without location)
- **No location in parentheses** - write "Intern Position" not "Intern Position (Paris)"
- **No redundant opening lines** - avoid stating "I am applying for..." since it's obvious
- **Traditional but distinctive openings** - start with a concise value proposition tied to the role, not a philosophical statement

### Tone & Language
- **Down to earth** - clear, direct, and comprehensible to finance professionals
- **Avoid verbosity** - concise sentences and plain wording over elaborate phrasing
- **Length is fine** - longer is acceptable when clear and meaningful; avoid complex sentences that reduce clarity
- **Confident but grounded** - highlight achievements without sounding inflated

### Opening Paragraph Structure
- **Position first** - state the role and company in the first sentence
- **Interest next** - brief reason for interest in the role/company
- **Hook last** - one strong, relevant skill or achievement to earn attention
